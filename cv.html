The career story

Where I've been & what it meant.

A timeline with actual notes — not just titles and dates.

Work Experience
March 2026 — Present
Senior Manager, Product Management & AI Business Transformation
Norlys · Copenhagen

Norlys is Denmark's largest integrated energy and telecom group — 1.7M customers, 4,500+ employees. I lead product management practices and work on how AI is reshaping the way we operate and build things.

Real talk: I got this role because I'd spent the previous year watching our product practices up close, getting quietly frustrated, and apparently talking about it too much — so they gave me a job to fix it. I got my PSPO I Scrum certification before starting because I had imposter syndrome and needed to feel ready. Turns out you still feel like an imposter after. But at least I know what a sprint is.

May 2025 — March 2026
Manager, Strategy & Transformation (Channels)
Norlys · Copenhagen

My first in-house role after consulting. Worked on channel strategy and transformation across Norlys's distribution and customer-facing operations.

Going in-house after consulting is a culture shock nobody really prepares you for. As a consultant, you leave at the end of the engagement. In-house, you live with your decisions — and with the people you made them with. It's harder in a better way. I genuinely loved this job.

2024 — May 2025
Associate
Strategy& (PwC) · Copenhagen

Strategy consulting on corporate strategy, transformation, and operating model work for clients across financial services and energy. First job out of my Masters.

Strategy& is where I learned how to structure a problem, how to present to people who don't have time for your full analysis, and how to work fast without cutting corners on quality. Also where I confirmed that I preferred building things over advising on them — which eventually pulled me toward Norlys.

2022 — 2024
Student Assistant
[Company] · Denmark

Worked as a student assistant for two years while finishing my Masters. Research, analysis, and strategy support — first exposure to how decisions actually get made inside a real organisation.

The gap between strategy in textbooks and strategy in practice is enormous. This is where I started to understand that the hard part is never the analysis — it's getting people to do something with it.
