Where I come from

Son of boat refugees.

My parents came to Denmark as Vietnamese boat refugees. They left everything behind, arrived with almost nothing, and built a life through sheer stubbornness and hard work. That's the foundation I grew up on. I don't take opportunities lightly — because I've seen what it looks like when you truly start from zero.

Making them proud matters to me more than most things. Not in a stressed-out, people-pleasing way — but in the way that actually makes you work harder, show up more prepared, and genuinely try to earn your place everywhere you land.

It's probably also why I ended up in this career. I love learning. I love complexity. And I love the feeling of understanding something that was opaque before.

The career part

How I got here — faster than expected.

I finished my Masters in February 2024. Before that, I spent two years as a student assistant — which is when I got my first real taste of how strategy actually works inside organisations, as opposed to how textbooks say it does.

After graduation I joined Strategy& as an Associate — the strategy consulting arm of PwC. Good firm. Smart people. I learned how to structure a problem, how to present to senior stakeholders without completely falling apart, and how to work fast under pressure.

In May 2025 I joined Norlys as Manager for Strategy & Transformation in their channels team. Norlys is Denmark's largest integrated energy and telecom group — 1.7M customers, 4,500+ employees. Going from consultant to in-house is a different kind of hard, but a better kind.

From March 2026 I took on a new role as Senior Manager for Product Management and AI & Business Transformation. I got a PSPO I Scrum certification before starting because I had classic imposter syndrome and needed to feel like I belonged. Turns out you still feel like an imposter after. The certification just makes you a better-prepared imposter.

Outside work
